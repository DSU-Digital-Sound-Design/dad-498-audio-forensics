## In-Class Activity: Scenario Cards

**Instructions:** Break into groups of 2-3. For each scenario, discuss:
1. Which pillar applies? (Authenticity, Enhancement, or Interpretation)
2. What technique from today's lecture might help?
3. What's one question you'd ask before starting?

---

### Scenario 1: The Disputed 911 Call

A man is accused of making a threatening 911 call. He claims the recording was edited to remove context where he explained he was joking. The prosecution says the recording is complete and unaltered.

---

### Scenario 2: The Parking Garage Assault

A security camera captured audio of an alleged assault in a parking garage. The victim's words are obscured by echoing footsteps and HVAC noise. The defense wants to know exactly what was said.

---

### Scenario 3: The Alibi Recording

A defendant claims he was at home during a crime, offering a voice memo recorded on his phone as proof. The timestamp shows 9:47 PM. The prosecution questions whether the recording was actually made at that time and location.

---

## Debrief

- Which scenario seemed most straightforward? Most complex?
- Did any scenario involve more than one pillar?
